A loan application can feel like a black box: you submit your documents, wait,
and hope. Having sat on the other side of that desk for years, I want to open
the box a little. Here are the five things that most influence whether your
auto loan gets a clean approval.

## 1. Your credit score and history

Your credit score is the headline number, but lenders read the **whole story**
— on-time payments, existing EMIs, credit utilisation, and any past defaults. A
score in good standing widens your options and improves your rate.

**Tip:** Check your report before you apply and fix any errors. Disputes take
time you don't want to spend mid-purchase.

## 2. Income stability and repayment capacity

Lenders calculate a **fixed-obligation-to-income ratio** — how much of your
income already goes to EMIs. If adding this loan pushes that ratio too high,
approval gets harder regardless of your score.

## 3. Loan-to-value (LTV)

This is the loan amount as a percentage of the vehicle's value. A larger down
payment means a lower LTV, which lenders love — it reduces their risk and often
unlocks a better rate for you.

## 4. Documentation quality

You'd be surprised how many approvals stall on avoidable paperwork issues:
mismatched names, expired proofs, or incomplete income documents. A **complete,
accurate file** is the single biggest thing you control.

- Identity and address proof
- Income proof (salary slips / ITR / bank statements)
- Vehicle quotation / proforma invoice

## 5. Field and profile verification

For many files, a lender runs a **Field Investigation (FI)** or contact-point
verification (CPV) — confirming your residence and workplace. Be reachable, and
make sure the details on your form match reality.

## Putting it together

None of these are hurdles designed to trip you up — they're how a lender builds
confidence that the loan will be repaid comfortably. Get these five right and
you move from _"under review"_ to _"approved"_ far faster.
