India's shift to electric mobility is no longer a future story — it's happening
now, fastest of all in the **3-wheeler** segment where the running-cost savings
are impossible to ignore. Financing an EV, though, isn't quite the same as
financing a petrol or diesel vehicle.

## Why EV financing is different

- **Battery is the biggest cost.** A large share of an EV's price sits in the
  battery pack, which affects how lenders assess resale value and risk.
- **Resale data is still maturing.** Lenders price risk partly on how well a
  vehicle holds value. With EVs relatively new, some lenders are more
  conservative — and others, more forward-looking.
- **Incentives change the math.** Central and state subsidies, lower GST, and
  road-tax waivers can meaningfully reduce the amount you actually finance.

## The total cost of ownership advantage

Where EVs really win is **running cost**. For a commercial 3-wheeler operator,
the fuel-versus-electricity gap can cover a large part of the EMI on its own.

> For many drivers, the monthly electricity bill is a fraction of what they
> used to spend on fuel — which is exactly why the EMI feels affordable.

When you evaluate an EV loan, don't look at the EMI in isolation. Look at
**EMI minus the fuel savings** — that's your real monthly outflow.

## What lenders look for

Whether it's an EV or a conventional vehicle, the fundamentals of a healthy
loan file don't change:

1. Clear, verifiable income or business cash flow.
2. A clean repayment history.
3. A sensible loan-to-value ratio and down payment.
4. Complete, accurate documentation (this is where most delays happen).

## Where this is heading

Green finance is becoming a core product line, not a niche. Purpose-built
lenders now underwrite EVs with data and structures designed for them — which
means better rates and faster approvals for buyers than even a year ago.
